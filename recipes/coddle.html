Mam's Homemade Coddle

Description

Coddle is a staple dish from Dublin, I spent my teenage years living in Cavan and when I told people I was having coddle for dinner they would look at me as if I had 10 heads. A dish that's perfect for the winter months and always reminds me of home.

Coddle is very similar to a stew. I will be honest the dish doesn't look very appealing but it's one of my all time favourites and a dish I would defend with my life!

Ingredients

  • Pork Sausages
  • Irish bacon
  • Potatoes
  • Onions
  • Carrots
  • Chicken stock
  • Milk
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Heat a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add sausages and bacon to the Dutch oven; cook until bacon is crisp. Reserve 1 tablespoon drippings in Dutch oven; drain and discard remaining drippings. Crumble bacon; halve sausages.
  2. Heat reserved drippings, crumbled bacon, and sausages in Dutch oven over low heat. Add potatoes, onions and carrots; cook and stir until onions soften, 7 to 10 minutes
  3. Stir in stock and milk; bring to a simmer and cook until potatoes are fork-tender, 30 to 45 minutes. Season with salt and black pepper.
  4. Serve!
